*ABA in case suppletion

*ABA

  • Bobaljik (2012): Restrictions on adjective suppletion.
  • For the triple postive ~ comparative ~ superlative:
    • AAA: smart ~ smarter ~ smartest
    • ABB: good ~ better ~ best
    • ABC: bonus ~ melior ~ optimus
    • *AAB: good ~ gooder ~ best
    • *ABA: good ~ better ~ goodest

*ABA: Person

  • Moskal (2018): Restrictions on clusivity suppletion.
  • For the triple singular ~ exclusive ~ inclusive:
    • AAA: n̍ohi ~ n̍omi ~ n̍ode (Tobelo)
    • ABB: ten ~ ʔáré ~ ʔáŋ (Kera)
    • ABC: kuh ~ mih ~ tam (Hanunoo)
    • AAB: ne ~ nere ~ le (Terei)
    • *ABA: ki ~ nieve ~ kian

*ABA: Number

  • Smith et al (2019): Restrictions on number suppletion.
  • For the triple singular ~ plural ~ dual:
    • AAA: ray ~ rom ~ ræw (Awtuw)
    • ABB: iou ~ kimaha ~ kimrau (Kwamera)
    • ABC: ngo ~ me ~ qe (Bilua)
    • AAB: angeice ~ angate ~ nyido (Dehu)
    • *ABA: ki ~ nieve ~ kian

*ABA: Generalisations

  • *ABA consistently not found in suppletion triples.
  • All other patterns consistently found
    (*AAB in adjectives probably due to locality(Moskal 2015).
  • *ABA seems genuinely impossible to derive.
  • Case is no different.

*ABA: Case

  • Smith et al (2019): restrictions on case suppletion.
    • AAA: čun ~ čna ~ čaz (Lezgian)
    • ABB: ol ~ anɨ ~ aɣaa (Khakas)
    • ABC: zɨ ~ jä ~ as(ɨr) (Khinalugh)
    • AAB: narnaj ~ narnaj-(j)i ~ gunga (Wardaman)
  • Triple as given in Smith et al (2019):
    Case 1 ~ Case 2 ~ Case 3

Case 1 ~ Case 2 ~ Case 3

  • Categorising in this way to follow Marantz (1991).
  • Distinctions between:
    • Unmarked case: nominative, absolutive
    • Dependent case: accusative, ergative
    • Lexical case: dative, instrumental etc.
